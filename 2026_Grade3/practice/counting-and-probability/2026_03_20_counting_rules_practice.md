## 📝 Part 1: The Challenges

### **Q1. The Tournament Outfit**
Julian is playing in a chess tournament. He has **4 lucky shirts** and **3 lucky pairs of pants**. How many different ways can he choose one shirt and one pair of pants to wear?
* (A) 7  
* (B) 10  
* (C) 12  
* (D) 15  

### **Q2. The Snack Choice**
At the math club, there are **5 different kinds of apples** and **6 different kinds of pears**. If a student can pick only ONE piece of fruit, how many choices do they have?
* (A) 1  
* (B) 11  
* (C) 30  
* (D) 65  

### **Q3. The Grid Path**
How many different paths are there from point A to point B on a $2 \times 2$ grid if you can only move **Right** or **Down**?
* (A) 4  
* (B) 6  
* (C) 8  
* (D) 10  

### **Q4. The Secret Code**
A security code consists of **two letters** followed by **one digit** (0-9). How many different codes are possible?
* (A) $26 + 26 + 10$  
* (B) $26 \times 2 \times 10$  
* (C) $26 \times 26 \times 10$  
* (D) $26 \times 25 \times 10$  

### **Q5. The Overlapping Clubs**
In a class of 30 students, **15 students** are in the Chess Club and **12 students** are in the Math Club. If **5 students** are in BOTH clubs, how many students are in at least one of the two clubs?
* (A) 22  
* (B) 27  
* (C) 32  
* (D) 17  

### **Q6. The Missing Number**
How many 3-digit numbers (from 100 to 999) **do not** contain the digit '7'?
* (A) $8 \times 9 \times 9$  
* (B) $9 \times 10 \times 10$  
* (C) $9 \times 9 \times 9$  
* (D) $10 \times 10 \times 10 - 1$  

### **Q7. The Pizza Toppings**
A pizza shop offers **3 types of crust** and **4 types of toppings**. A customer must choose one crust and can choose **either zero, one, or two** toppings. How many different pizzas can be made?
* (A) 12  
* (B) 21  
* (C) 33  
* (D) 36  

### **Q8. The Photo Line-up (New!)**
Julian wants to take a picture of his **3 favorite Pokémon** (Pikachu, Bulbasaur, and Charmander) standing in a row. How many different ways can he arrange them for the photo?
* (A) 3  
* (B) 6  
* (C) 9  
* (D) 12  

### **Q9. The Divisibility Rule**
How many integers between 1 and 100 (inclusive) are divisible by **either 5 or 7**?
* (A) 30  
* (B) 31  
* (C) 32  
* (D) 34  

### **Q10. The Restricted Password**
A 3-digit number is formed using digits {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}. How many of these numbers have **at least one** repeated digit?
* (A) 25  
* (B) 60  
* (C) 65  
* (D) 125

| **Q1** | **(C) 12** | Product Rule: $4 \times 3$ |
| **Q2** | **(B) 11** | Sum Rule: $5 + 6$ |
| **Q3** | **(B) 6** | Sum Rule Waterfall (Paths from Left + Paths from Top) |
| **Q4** | **(C) 6760**| Product Rule: $26 \times 26 \times 10$ |
| **Q5** | **(A) 22** | PIE Rule: $15 + 12 - 5$ |
| **Q6** | **(A) 648** | Product Rule: $8 \times 9 \times 9$ (1st digit cannot be 0 or 7) |
| **Q7** | **(C) 33** | $3 \text{ crusts} \times (1 \text{ zero} + 4 \text{ one} + 6 \text{ two toppings})$ |
| **Q8** | **(B) 6** | **Factorial Magic: $3! = 3 \times 2 \times 1$** |
| **Q9** | **(C) 32** | PIE Rule: $20 + 14 - 2$ (Subtract multiples of 35) |
| **Q10**| **(C) 65** | Complementary: Total ($5^3$) - No Repeats ($5 \times 4 \times 3$) |
