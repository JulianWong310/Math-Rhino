# 🎓 The Graphical Method & Point-Plotting
### (函数图象的画法：描点法)

The **Graphical Method (图象法)** is the most visual way to represent a function. It allows us to see the "behavior" of a function on a **Coordinate Plane (直角坐标系)**.

---

## 1. The 3-Step "Point-Plotting" Method (作图三部曲)

To draw the graph of any function, follow these three essential steps:

### ① Create a Table (列表)
Pick several values for the **Independent Variable** $x$, and calculate the corresponding values for the **Dependent Variable (因变量)** $y$.
* *Note:* Choose values that are easy to calculate and plot (like $-2, -1, 0, 1, 2$).

### ② Plot the Points (描点)
For each pair of $(x, y)$ in your table, mark a point on the coordinate plane. 
* The $x$-value tells you the horizontal position.
* The $y$-value tells you the vertical position.

### ③ Connect the Dots (连线)
Join the points in order (from left to right) using a **smooth line (直线)** or a **smooth curve (光滑曲线)**.

---

## 2. Example Walkthrough: Graphing $y = x + 0.5$

### Step 1: Table
| $x$ | -2 | -1 | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$y$** | **-1.5** | **-0.5** | **0.5** | **1.5** | **2.5** |

### Step 2 & 3: Plot and Connect


*As you can see, the points form a perfectly **Straight Line (直线)**.*

---

## 3. Advanced Example: Graphing $y = x^2$

For functions like $y = x^2$, the points do not form a straight line. We must connect them with a curve.

| $x$ | -2 | -1 | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$y$** | **4** | **1** | **0** | **1** | **4** |



*This U-shaped curve is called a **Parabola (抛物线)**. Remember to draw it smoothly, not with sharp corners!*

# 📝 Practice Problems 

**Problem 1: Drawing Task**
Follow the 3-step method to draw the graph of the function **$y = 2x - 1$**.
1. Create a table for $x \in \{-2, -1, 0, 1, 2\}$.
2. Plot the points on your graph paper.
3. Connect them. Is it a line or a curve?

**Problem 2: Reading the Graph**
Look at the graph of $y = x + 0.5$ you drew earlier.
1. When $x = 1.5$, what is the value of $y$ on the line?
2. When $y = 0$, what is the value of $x$?

**Problem 3: The "Speed-Time" Graph**
A car starts from a standstill and increases its speed. The graph shows the relationship between **Time ($t$)** and **Speed ($v$)**.
* If the graph is a **horizontal line (水平线)**, what does it tell you about the speed?
  * (A) The car is stopping.
  * (B) The car is moving at a constant speed (速度不变).
  * (C) The car is getting faster.

---

# 🔑 Answers and Logic 

1. **Problem 1:** * Table values: $(-2, -5), (-1, -3), (0, -1), (1, 1), (2, 3)$. 
   * Result: A **Straight Line**.

2. **Problem 2:**
   * (1) $y = 1.5 + 0.5 = \mathbf{2}$.
   * (2) $0 = x + 0.5 \implies x = \mathbf{-0.5}$.

3. **Problem 3:**
   * **(B) Constant speed**. 
   * *Logic:* If the line is horizontal, it means as time $t$ increases, the speed $v$ stays exactly the same.
