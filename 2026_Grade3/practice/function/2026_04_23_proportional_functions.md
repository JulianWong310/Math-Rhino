# Lecture: Proportional Functions 

> **Core Concept:** A function of the form **$y = kx$** (where $k$ is a constant and $k \neq 0$) is called a **proportional function**. The constant $k$ is called the **coefficient of proportionality**.

---

## ЁЯУШ Part 1: Definition & Identification

### 1. The "Shell" of a Proportional Function
**Dependent variable** = $k$ ├Ч **Independent variable**
$$y = kx \quad (k \neq 0)$$

### ЁЯЫа Example 1: Identifying Proportional Functions
Determine whether each function is a proportional function.

1. $y = -x$  
   **Answer:** Yes ($k = -1$)
2. $y = \frac{x}{3}$  
   **Answer:** Yes ($k = \frac{1}{3}$)
3. $y = \frac{1}{3}x$  
   **Answer:** Yes ($k = \frac{1}{3}$)
4. $y = 8x$  
   **Answer:** Yes ($k = 8$)
5. $y = 3x + 2$  
   **Answer:** No (It has a constant term $+2$)
6. $y = \frac{2}{x}$  
   **Answer:** No ($x$ is in the denominator, not to the power of 1)
7. $y = kx^2$  
   **Answer:** No ($x$ is squared)
8. $y = x^2$  
   **Answer:** No ($x$ is squared)

## ЁЯМН Part 2: Real-World Applications

### ЁЯЫа Example 2: Real-World Proportional Relationships
Determine whether the function described is a proportional function.

1. **High-speed train:** A train travels at 300 km/h. Distance $y$ vs. time $t$.  
   **Answer:** Yes. $y = 300t$ ($k = 300$)
2. **Circumference:** Circumference $C$ vs. radius $r$.  
   **Answer:** Yes. $C = 2\pi r$ ($k = 2\pi$)
3. **Density:** Mass $m$ of iron (density 7.8 g/cm┬│) vs. volume $V$.  
   **Answer:** Yes. $m = 7.8V$ ($k = 7.8$)
4. **Thickness:** Total thickness $h$ of $n$ workbooks (each 0.5 cm thick).  
   **Answer:** Yes. $h = 0.5n$ ($k = 0.5$)
5. **Freezing:** An object at 10┬░C decreases by 2┬░C per minute. Temperature $T$ vs. time $t$.  
   **Answer:** No. $T = 10 -2t$ (It has a constant term $+ 10$)

## ЁЯФв Part 3: Solving for Coefficients

### ЁЯЫа Example 3: Finding Unknowns
1. If $y = (m - 4)x$ is a proportional function, what is the condition for $m$?  
   **Answer:** $m \neq 4$ (The coefficient $k$ must not be 0).
2. If $y = 5x^{3m-2}$ is a proportional function, find $m$.  
   **Answer:** $3m - 2 = 1 \Rightarrow 3m = 3 \Rightarrow m = 1$.
3. Given $y = (m - 3)x^{m^2-8}$, for what value(s) of $m$ is $y$ a proportional function?  
   **Answer:** Step 1: $m^2 - 8 = 1 \Rightarrow m^2 = 9 \Rightarrow m = 3$ or $m = -3$.  
   Step 2: $m - 3 \neq 0 \Rightarrow m \neq 3$.  
   Final Result: **$m = -3$**.

### ЁЯЫа Example 4: Always Proportional?
Which of the following is **always** a proportional function (where $a$ is any constant)?  
A. $y = (a + 1)x$  
B. $y = \frac{1}{a}x$  
C. $y = \frac{x}{a^2}$  
D. $y = \frac{x}{a^2 + 1}$  
**Answer:** **D**. (Since $a^2 + 1$ is always $\geq 1$, the denominator is never zero, making $k = \frac{1}{a^2+1}$ a valid non-zero constant).

## ЁЯТб Summary Checklist

| Check | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coefficient $k$** | $k \neq 0$ |
| **Exponent on $x$** | Must be exactly **1** |
| **Constant term** | Must be **0** (no added constants) |
| **Graph Shape** | A **straight line** passing through the origin **(0,0)** |
