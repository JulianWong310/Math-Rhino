# 🎓Three Ways to Represent a Function

---

## 1. The Table Method 
This method uses a **Table** to list the inputs ($x$) and their matching outputs ($y$) directly.

* **Pros:** It’s very clear! You can see the exact values at a glance.
* **Cons:** It only shows a few points, not the whole story.

| $x$ (Input) | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| $y$ (Output) | 2 | 4 | 6 | 8 | 10 |

> **Key Word:** **Table Method**

---

## 2. The Graphical Method 
We draw the relationship on a **Coordinate Plane**. Each pair of $(x, y)$ becomes a point on the graph.

* **Pros:** You can see the "trend" (is it going up? is it a curve?).
* **Cons:** It’s hard to get an exact number just by looking at the picture.

[Image of a linear function graph on a coordinate plane]

> **Key Word:** **Graphical Method**

---

## 3. The Algebraic Method / Formula Method 
This is the most powerful way! We use an **Equation** or **Formula** to describe the rule.

* **Pros:** You can calculate the output for *any* input, even huge numbers.
* **Example:** $y = 2x + 1$
    * If $x = 100$, then $y = 2(100) + 1 = 201$.

> **Key Word:** **Algebraic Method / Formula Method**

---

## 🌟 Summary: Which one to use?
* Want to see exact values quickly? Use the **Table**.
* Want to see the shape and movement? Use the **Graph**.
* Want to do complex calculations? Use the **Formula**.
