# Measurement of Length and Time

## 1. Why do we measure? 
In daily life, we use our senses to judge size or time. But look at **Fig 1.1-1**: sometimes our eyes trick us! To be accurate in science, we need **standard instruments** (rulers, clocks, thermometers) and **units**.

---

## 2. Measurement of Length
To measure anything, we first need a **Unit**.

### The International System of Units (SI)
Long ago, people used "feet" or "cubits," but everyone's feet are different sizes! To make international trade and science easier, we created the **SI System**.
*   **Base Unit for Length:** The **meter (m)**.

### Conversion Table (Power of 10)
| Unit | Symbol | Relationship to Meter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Kilometer | km | $1 km = 10^3 m$ |
| Decimeter | dm | $1 dm = 10^{-1} m$ |
| Centimeter | cm | $1 cm = 10^{-2} m$ |
| Millimeter | mm | $1 mm = 10^{-3} m$ |
| Micrometer | μm | $1 μm = 10^{-6} m$ |
| Nanometer | nm | $1 nm = 10^{-9} m$ |





### How to use a Ruler (The Right Way!)
1.  **Check the Scale:** Look for the "Zero" line and the **Scale Interval** (the smallest value the ruler can show).
2.  **Placement:** The ruler must be parallel to the object. Don't tilt it!
3.  **The Eye Rule:** Your line of sight must be **perpendicular** to the ruler's surface. 
4.  **Recording:** Always include the **number** AND the **unit**. (10 is just a number; 10cm is a measurement!)

---

## 3. Measurement of Time
*   **Base Unit for Time:** The **second (s)**.
*   **Other Units:** Hours (h), Minutes (min).
    *   $1 h = 60 min$
    *   $1 min = 60 s$



---

## 4. Errors (The "Oops" Factor)
In Physics, there is no such thing as a "perfect" measurement. 
*   **Error:** The difference between your measured value and the true value. 
    *   *Can we eliminate it?* No.
    *   *Can we reduce it?* Yes! By taking multiple measurements and calculating the **average**.
*   **Mistake:** This is different from an error. A mistake (like reading the wrong number) can be avoided. Errors cannot.

## 📝 Practice Answers 

### Task 1: Unit Conversion Challenge
1. **$5 \text{ km} = 5000 \text{ m}$** > *Logic:* $1 \text{ km} = 10^3 \text{ m}$. So, $5 \times 1000 = 5000$.
2. **$20 \text{ cm} = 0.2 \text{ m}$**
   > *Logic:* $1 \text{ cm} = 10^{-2} \text{ m}$. So, $20 / 100 = 0.2$.
3. **$1.5 \text{ h} = 5400 \text{ s}$**
   > *Logic:* $1 \text{ hour} = 60 \text{ min} \times 60 \text{ sec} = 3600 \text{ s}$. $1.5 \times 3600 = 5400$.

### Task 3: The Pendulum Experiment
* **Answer:** You should time **10 swings** and then divide the total time by 10.
* **Why?** This reduces **Measurement Error**. Humans have a reaction time (about 0.2s). If you only measure 1 swing, that 0.2s is a huge error. If you measure 10 swings, that same 0.2s error is spread out, making the result for 1 swing much more accurate.
