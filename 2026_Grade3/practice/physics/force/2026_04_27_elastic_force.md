#  Elastic Force


## Part 1: Elastic Force

### Elasticity and Plasticity

**Elasticity:** The property of an object to return to its original shape after the deforming force is removed.

**Plasticity:** The property of an object to remain deformed and not automatically return to its original shape after the deforming force is removed.

### Types of Deformation

**Elastic Deformation:** Deformation that disappears completely after the external force is removed.

**Plastic Deformation:** Deformation that remains after the external force is removed.

### Elastic Limit

When the deformation of an elastic object exceeds a certain limit, the object cannot return to its original shape even after the force is removed. This limit is called the **elastic limit**.

### Examples

| Elastic | Plastic |
|---------|---------|
| Spring | Dough |
| Rubber band | Playdough / Plasticine |

## Part 2: Elastic Force

### Definition

**Elastic force** is the force generated by an object due to elastic deformation. It occurs between objects that are:
1. In direct contact
2. Undergoing elastic deformation

### Magnitude of Elastic Force

- Depends on the degree of elastic deformation.
- Within the elastic limit: the greater the deformation, the greater the elastic force.
- When deformation disappears, the elastic force also disappears.

### Direction of Elastic Force

- Opposite to the direction of the elastic deformation.

## Part 3: Spring Dynamometer

### Principle

Within the elastic limit, the greater the force applied to a spring, the longer the spring's extension.

> **Spring extension is proportional to the applied force.**

### How to Use a Spring Dynamometer

1. **Check the range and scale division** (measurement range and smallest division).
2. **Zero the instrument** before use.
3. **Align the axis** of the dynamometer with the line of action of the force being measured. Make sure the pointer does not rub against the housing.
4. **Read the scale** with your eyes level with the刻度板.
5. **Do not exceed** the measurement range of the dynamometer.

### Brief Explanations

- **Q2:** Contact alone is not enough; elastic deformation is required.
- **Q3:** Magnetic force is a non-contact force, not elastic.
- **Q5:** The deformation of the board is caused by the ball's pressure, not by the board's own elastic force (causation is reversed).
- **Q7:** A stationary spring dynamometer under equal opposite forces reads the magnitude of one of the forces (5 N).
- **Q8:** A spring dynamometer under equal opposite forces reads the magnitude of one force (2 N).
- **Q9:** From 5 N → 2 cm extension (12 – 10). For 10 N (double), extension doubles to 4 cm, so length = 14 cm.
- **Q10:** When upside down, the spring's own weight adds to the reading, so the actual object weight is **less than** the reading.
