## Part 1: Force and Its Effects

**Concept**: Force is an interaction between two objects. It is generally represented by the symbol **F**.

**Unit**: In the International System of Units (SI), force is measured in **Newtons**, abbreviated as **N**. (Every force has a **source object** and a **target object**).

**Effects of Force**:
1. Force can change the shape of an object (cause deformation).
2. Force can change the motion state of an object.

---

## Part 2: The Three Elements of Force and Force Diagrams

**The Three Elements of Force**: The **magnitude**, **direction**, and **point of application** of a force. All three elements affect the outcome of the force.

**Force Diagram**: A diagram where a line segment with an arrow is drawn on the target object, starting from the point of application and pointing in the direction of the force. This is called a force diagram. (It must clearly indicate magnitude, direction, and point of application).

### Further Understanding of Force:

1. **Forces are mutual** – If object A exerts a force on object B, then object B exerts an equal and opposite force on object A.
2. **Force cannot exist independently** – It always requires two objects.
3. **Forces occur in pairs** – The mutual forces arise and disappear at the same time.
4. **Contact is not always necessary** – Forces can act without contact (e.g., gravity, magnetism), and contact does not always mean a force is present.

### Diagram Examples:
- Example 1: Pulling a cart with a force of 100 N
  - **Point of application**: Where the force is applied.
  - **Direction**: The direction of the pull/push.
  - **Magnitude**: 100 N

**Explanation for Question 10**:  
- Phenomena ① and ④ show changes in motion (dust starts moving, basketball reverses direction).  
- Phenomena ② and ③ only show changes in shape (deformation), not motion state.

## Summary:

- Force is an interaction measured in Newtons (N).
- Forces can change shape or motion.
- Always specify the **magnitude**, **direction**, and **point of application**.
- Forces are mutual, always come in pairs, and do not require direct contact.
