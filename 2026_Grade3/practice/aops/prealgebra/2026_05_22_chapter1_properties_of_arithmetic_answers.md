# 🔑 Answer Key & Explanations

## Part 1: Basic Properties of Arithmetic
* **Q1: B** — The commutative property states that order doesn't matter for addition: $a + b = b + a$.
* **Q2: C** — Grouping order changes describe the associative property: $(a + b) + c = a + (b + c)$.
* **Q3: B** — Adding zero leaves a number unchanged: $a + 0 = a$.
* **Q4: C** — Order doesn't matter for multiplication: $ab = ba$.
* **Q5: B** — Grouping changes for multiplication describe the associative property: $(ab)c = a(bc)$.
* **Q6: D** — Multiplying by $1$ keeps the identity of the number: $1a = a$.
* **Q7: B** — Distribution means multiplying the outer term with every term inside the parentheses: $a(b + c) = ab + ac$ and $(b + c)a = ba + ca$.
* **Q8: C** — Adding a number to its negative opposite always yields zero: $-a + a = 0$.
* **Q9: B** — Multiplying a nonzero number by its reciprocal yields one: $\frac{1}{a} \cdot a = 1$.

## Part 2: Definitions of Subtraction and Division
* **Q10: B** — Subtraction is defined as adding the opposite: $a - b = a + (-b)$.
* **Q11: C** — Division is defined as multiplying by the reciprocal: $a \div b = a \cdot \frac{1}{b}$.

## Part 3: Properties of Negation
* **Q12: C** — The negative of a negative is positive: $-(-a) = a$.
* **Q13: C** — The negative sign outside applies to both numbers inside: $-(a + b) = (-a) + (-b)$.
* **Q14: B** — Any number multiplied by zero is zero: $0a = 0$.
* **Q15: C** — Multiplying by $-1$ flips the sign: $(-1)a = -a$.
* **Q16: D** — When multiplying a positive and a negative, the result must be negative: $(-a)b = -(ab)$. Thus, option D is incorrect.
* **Q17: B** — Two negative numbers multiplied together produce a positive outcome: $(-a)(-b) = ab$.

## Part 4: Properties Involving Subtraction
* **Q18: C** — $0 - a = 0 + (-a) = -a$.
* **Q19: B** — Any number subtracted from itself leaves nothing: $a - a = 0$.
* **Q20: A** — Subtracting zero changes nothing: $a - 0 = a$.
* **Q21: C** — Subtracting a negative turns into adding a positive: $a - (-b) = a + b$.
* **Q22: B** — Factoring out the negative turns both terms positive inside the parentheses: $-a - b = -(a + b)$.
* **Q23: C** — $-(a - b) = -a - (-b) = -a + b = b - a$.
* **Q24: A** — Multiplication distributes over subtraction cleanly: $a(b - c) = ab - ac$ and $(b - c)a = ba - ca$.

## Part 5: Properties Involving Reciprocals and Division
* **Q25: C** — Flipping a fraction upside down twice returns it to its original form: $\frac{1}{\frac{1}{a}} = a$.
* **Q26: B** — A negative sign in the denominator can be pulled out in front: $\frac{1}{-a} = -\frac{1}{a}$.
* **Q27: C** — The reciprocal of a product is the product of the reciprocals: $\frac{1}{ab} = \frac{1}{a} \cdot \frac{1}{b}$.
* **Q28: D** — Zero divided by any valid nonzero number is zero: $0 \div a = 0$.
* **Q29: B** — Any nonzero number divided by itself is one: $a \div a = 1$.
* **Q30: C** — Dividing by $1$ does not change the value: $a \div 1 = a$.
* **Q31: B** — By definition, $1 \div x = \frac{1}{x}$.
* **Q32: C** — Dividing by $\frac{1}{b}$ is equivalent to multiplying by $b$: $a \div \frac{1}{b} = ab$.
* **Q33: A** — A single negative sign anywhere in a division makes the whole quotient negative.
* **Q34: B** — Two negative signs cancel each other out in division: $(-a) \div (-b) = a \div b$.
* **Q35: A** — The shared factor $a$ cancels out from both sides: $(ab) \div (ac) = b \div c$.
* **Q36: A** — Division distributes over a sum from the right side: $(a + b) \div c = a \div c + b \div c$.
* **Q37: B** — Division distributes over a difference from the right side: $(a - b) \div c = a \div c - b \div c$.

## Part 6: Arithmetic Warnings (Common Pitfalls)
* **Q38: C** — Subtraction is neither commutative nor associative. Order and grouping matter: $a - b \neq b - a$ and $a - (b - c) \neq (a - b) - c$.
* **Q39: C** — Division by zero is mathematically undefined. You must ensure the divisor is nonzero before calculating.
* **Q40: D** — Division is neither commutative nor associative: $a \div b \neq b \div a$ and $a \div (b \div c) \neq (a \div b) \div c$.
* **Q41: B** — You can distribute a dividend over a single divisor, but you cannot break apart a sum in the divisor: $a \div (b + c) \neq a \div b + a \div c$.
