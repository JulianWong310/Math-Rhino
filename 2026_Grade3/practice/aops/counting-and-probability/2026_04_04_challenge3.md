### Challenge 3
</div>

 For every problem, ask yourself: **"Does the order matter?"** and **"Can we repeat?"**

---

#### Level 1: The Race vs. The Team

**Problem A (The Race):** 5 kids are in a running race. In how many different ways can we award the **Gold, Silver, and Bronze** medals?

**Problem B (The Pizza):** 5 kids are at a party. Julian needs to pick **3 kids** to go get pizza for the whole group. How many different groups of 3 kids can he choose?

---

#### Level 2: The Lock vs. The Plate

**Problem A (The Bike Lock):** A bike lock has **3 slots**, and each slot can be any digit from **1 to 5**. You **CAN** use the same number more than once (for example, 1-1-2). How many total codes are possible?

**Problem B (The Club ID):** A club ID has **3 slots** using digits **1 to 5**, but you **CANNOT** use the same digit more than once in a single ID. How many different IDs are possible?

---

#### Level 3: The "Even Code" Challenge

**The Puzzle:** Julian is making a 3-digit secret code using the digits **{1, 2, 3, 4}**. 
1. The code must be **EVEN** (it must end in 2 or 4).
2. No digits can be repeated.

How many different secret codes can Julian make? 
*(Hint: Think about two different cases—one where it ends in 2, and one where it ends in 4.)*

---

#### Level 4: The "Captain & Crew"

**The Scenario:** In a chess club of **6 members**:
1. First, Julian must pick **1 Captain**.
2. Then, he must pick **2 more members** (from the remaining people) to be on the general team.

How many different ways can he form this 3-person group?

---

#### Level 5: The "MATH" Hacker

**The Task:** 1. How many different 4-letter **"words"** can you make using all the letters in the word **"MATH"**? (You must use each letter exactly once).
2. Out of all those words, how many of them must **START** with the letter **M**?
