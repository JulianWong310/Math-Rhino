#### Level 1: The Prime Mirror (The "7" Trap)

**Definition:** An **Emirp** is a prime that is still prime when reversed (like $17 \leftrightarrow 71$).
**The Puzzle:** What is the **largest** two-digit prime number that is **NOT** an emirp?
*(Hint: Start from 97 and count backwards. Check the 90s, then the 80s... one of them is a "Fake Prime"!)*

---

#### Level 2: Factorial DNA Cleanup (The Double N)

**The Task:** Clean up this "Fraction Ghost" so there are no exclamation marks (!) left:
$$\frac{(N+2)!}{(N)! \times (N+1)}$$
*(Hint: Break down the $(N+2)!$ step-by-step. What happens when you hit $(N+1)$?)*

---

#### Level 3: The "Plus One" Equation

**The Task:** Find the value of $n$ if:
$$n \cdot n! + n! = 120$$
---

#### Level 4: The Square Factor Hunt (The "6!" Edition)

**The Task:** What is the **greatest perfect square** that is a factor of $6!$?

#### Level 5: The "Sum of Factorials" Mystery

**The Task:** There is only one other number like 145. Find the **three-digit integer** that is equal to the sum of the factorials of its digits.
*Logic Check:* If the number is $ABC$, then $A! + B! + C! = ABC$.

---

#### Level 6: The "B-C" License Plate

**The Task:** A secret robot club issues IDs that follow this rule:
* **Slot 1:** Must be the letter **B**, **C**, or **D**.
* **Slots 2-6:** Must be any **5 digits** (0-9).
**Question:** How many different Robot IDs can be created?

---

#### Level 7: The Rival Captains (The "No-Together" Rule)

**The Scenario:** Julian is picking a team of **3 people** from a group of **6 friends**. 
However, two of the friends, **Alex** and **Ben**, are rival captains and **REFUSE** to be on the same team together.
**The Question:** How many different 3-person teams can Julian form that do **NOT** have both Alex and Ben at the same time?

---
**Strategic Hints:**

* **Strategy A (The Subtraction Hack):** 1. Calculate the **TOTAL** number of ways to pick 3 people from 6 (Ignore the rule for a second).
    2. Calculate how many "Illegal Teams" contain **BOTH** Alex and Ben. (Hint: If Alex and Ben are already on the team, you only need to pick **1 more person** from the remaining 4).
    3. Subtract: $Total - Illegal = Winning Answer$.

* **Strategy B (The Case Study):**
    1. Case 1: Teams with **Alex** (but no Ben).
    2. Case 2: Teams with **Ben** (but no Alex).
    3. Case 3: Teams with **Neither** Alex nor Ben.
    4. Add them up!
