**brace**（支撑、稳固）：To press something firmly to keep it steady.
* He had to brace his feet against the wall to push the heavy sofa.
* You should brace the shelf with strong screws so it doesn't fall.

**skiff**（小独木舟、小帆船）：A small light boat for rowing or sailing.
* The old fisherman spent the whole afternoon fixing his small skiff.
* They rowed the skiff across the quiet lake to reach the other side.

**severely**（严重地）：In a very bad or serious way.
* The plants were severely damaged by the sudden frost last night.
* The teacher severely warned the students not to cheat during exams.

**resemble**（像、与……相似）：To look like someone or something.
* The two sisters resemble each other so much that people always mix them up.
* These dark clouds look scary and resemble a giant monster in the sky.

**swell**（肿胀、膨胀）：To become larger or rounder from pressure or injury.
* If you get stung by a bee, your skin will quickly swell up.
* The heavy rain caused the little river to swell and flood the road.

**endure**（忍受、持久）：To suffer something difficult, painful, or unpleasant in a patient way.
* The travelers had to endure freezing temperatures during their trek across the mountains.
* She learned to endure the long hours of training to reach her goals.

**relentless**（持续不断的、无情的）：Not stopping or becoming less strong.
* The runner kept moving forward despite the relentless heat of the sun.
* The baby's relentless crying kept the parents awake for the entire night.

**throbbed with**（抽痛、阵阵跳动）：To beat or pulse strongly, often with pain or emotion.
* His leg throbbed with a sharp pain after he fell off the bicycle.
* The crowded concert hall throbbed with the loud beat of the drums.

**majestic**（雄伟庄严的）：Very large and beautiful in a dignified way.
* The majestic eagle soared high above the snow-covered mountains.
* We stood in front of the majestic palace and took many photos.

**phantom**（幻影、幽灵）：A ghost or something that seems real but is not.
* The nervous driver thought he saw a phantom crossing the dark road.
* He could still feel a phantom pain in his arm even after it healed.

**elongated**（瘦长的、拉长的）：Made longer and thinner than usual.
* The shadows of the trees became elongated as the sun began to set.
* The artist drew a strange creature with an elongated neck and big eyes.

**bill**（长吻、鸟喙）：The beak of a bird, especially a long, flat one.
* The duck dipped its flat bill into the muddy water to search for food.
* The seagull held a shiny little fish tightly in its sharp bill.

**brilliantly**（鲜艳明亮地、出色地）：In a very bright or clever way.
* The stars shone brilliantly in the clear night sky far away from the city.
* The young pianist played the difficult song brilliantly and won first prize.

**awe**（敬畏、惊叹）：A feeling of wonder and respect mixed with fear.
* The children watched the colorful fireworks display in complete awe.
* The grand scale of the ancient pyramid filled the tourists with awe.

**butcher**（割裂、屠宰）：To kill animals for meat, or to ruin something badly.
* The local market sells fresh meat because they butcher the animals daily.
* If you don't practice the song, you will butcher it on stage tomorrow.

**determination**（决心、毅力）：The quality of continuing to try even when things are hard.
* With hard work and determination, she finally learned how to code in Python.
* His determination to finish the race helped him cross the finish line.

**grip**（紧握、抓紧）：To hold something very tightly.
* Make sure you grip the steering wheel firmly when driving in the rain.
* The little boy lost his grip on the balloon, and it flew up into the sky.

**noble**（高贵的、高尚的）：Having high moral qualities, or a person of high birth.
* Saving the injured puppy from the busy street was a very noble act.
* The knight came from a noble family and always fought with great honor.

**grim**（坚定的、严峻的）：Serious, gloomy, or unpleasant.
* The doctor had a grim expression on his face when he delivered the bad news.
* The soldiers faced a grim situation as the winter storm got worse.
