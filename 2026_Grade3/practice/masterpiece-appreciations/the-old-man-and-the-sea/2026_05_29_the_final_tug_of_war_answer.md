### **Part 2: Open-Response Exemplar**

> **Sample Answer:**
 In this passage, the theme of resilience is clearly demonstrated through Santiago's refusal to give up despite his overwhelming physical suffering. Even though his hands are "stiff and severely cut" resembling "dried, twisted vines," and he is so exhausted that "black spots danced before his eyes," his actions prove his unyielding spirit. Instead of letting go of the line, he actively fights his weakness by rubbing cold sea water on his neck to stay conscious and pulling the line "inch by inch." Furthermore, his thoughts reinforce this resilience when he mentally commands himself, "You have to last. Don’t even think of quitting," and declares that "a man can be destroyed but not defeated." These specific details show that true resilience is defined by a person's mental determination to endure when their physical body has reached its absolute limit.
