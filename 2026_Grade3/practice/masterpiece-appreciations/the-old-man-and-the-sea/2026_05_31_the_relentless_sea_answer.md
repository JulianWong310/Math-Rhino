# *The Old Man and the Sea* — Answer Key & Detailed Explanations

### Part 1: Multiple-Choice Questions — Answer Key & Explanations

**1. In paragraph 1, what does the phrase "built for eating" suggest about the shark?**
* **Correct Answer:** **B) The shark was naturally designed to be a dangerous predator.**
* **Explanation:** The phrase "built for eating" is a descriptive metaphor showing that the shark's entire physical structure and instincts are made for hunting and consuming prey. Options A and D contradict the shark's aggressive nature, and Option C is incorrect because the text does not compare its physical size to previous sharks in this paragraph.

**2. Which word is closest in meaning to the word "mutilated" as used in paragraph 1?**
* **Correct Answer:** **C) Heavily damaged**
* **Explanation:** In the context of paragraph 1, the text describes the prize fish trailing behind the skiff as being "already severely mutilated by the first attack." This means the fish's body was torn apart and deeply ruined by the previous shark. Therefore, "heavily damaged" is the most accurate synonym.

**3. According to paragraph 2, how did Santiago prepare physically to strike the shark?**
* **Correct Answer:** **B) He braced his feet and ignored the throbbing pain in his hands.**
* **Explanation:** This is a direct retrieval question. Paragraph 2 explicitly states: *“He braced his feet against the floorboards, raised his harpoon with raw hands that throbbed with pain, and struck down...”* Option A is incorrect because he stood on the floorboards, not the bow. Option C is factually incorrect as he was completely alone, and Option D is the opposite of what happened, as he did not rest.

**4. What does paragraph 3 reveal about Santiago's character?**
* **Correct Answer:** **C) He possesses an unyielding spirit despite knowing more difficulties lie ahead.**
* **Explanation:** At the end of paragraph 3, even though Santiago's body is aching and he knows more sharks are drawn to the scent of blood, the text notes that *"looking out at the vast, empty horizon, he refused to give up."* This directly demonstrates an unyielding, resilient spirit. He is neither easily frightened (Option A) nor looking to abandon his work (Option B).

**5. In paragraph 5, why does Santiago continue to fight the sharks even though he knows the fish cannot be saved?**
* **Correct Answer:** **B) He is driven by pride and a need to test his own endurance.**
* **Explanation:** Paragraph 5 clarifies that the battle has shifted purposes: *“He knew the fight was no longer about saving the meat; it was a pure test of human endurance...”* Earlier in paragraph 4, it also mentions that a *"fierce spark of pride"* kept him upright. This proves he is fighting for his dignity and endurance rather than trying to harvest the sharks (Option A) or waiting for an imaginary rescue boat (Option C).

### Part 2: Open-Response Questions — Exemplar Answer

**6. Based on the passage, explain why the old man is facing a "grim situation" out on the ocean. Use specific details from paragraphs 3 and 4 to support your answer.**

#### **Sample Answer**

Based on paragraphs 3 and 4, the old man is facing a grim situation because his physical body is failing him, his weapons are nearly gone, the environment is turning hostile, and more predators are attacking at the same time. 

* **Physical Exhaustion and Injuries:** According to paragraph 3, Santiago’s body is "aching," his throat burns with a "fierce thirst," and his palms are "bleeding" from holding the rope. In paragraph 4, his body is described as "nearly broken" and his muscles are completely "stiff."
* **Lack of Proper Weapons:** Paragraph 3 states that Santiago realizes he only has "a single oar left to use as a weapon," meaning he no longer has his strong harpoon to fight back effectively.
* **Harsh Environmental Changes:** As the sun sets, the water turns an "ominous red," and the wind picks up, bringing the "cold chill of the approaching night," which adds severe cold to his exhaustion.
* **Overwhelming Number of Predators:** Instead of fighting just one enemy, paragraph 4 highlights that "two more phantoms of the deep" (sharks) appear together. They attack from a direction he "could not easily defend," making it impossible for him to save his catch. 

In conclusion, the combination of his bleeding hands, the cold night, having only a single oar, and multiple sharks attacking simultaneously creates a truly desperate and grim situation for Santiago.
