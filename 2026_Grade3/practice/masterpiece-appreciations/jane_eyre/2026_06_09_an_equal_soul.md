# Excerpt from *Jane Eyre*

### An Equal Soul

**[1]** Jane shook herself loose from his hand and faced him directly. "Do you think I am an automaton?—a machine without feelings? and can bear to have my morsel of bread snatched from my lips, and my drop of living water dashed from my cup? Do you think, because I am poor, obscure, plain, and little, I am soulless and heartless? You think wrong!—I have as much soul as you,—and full as much heart! And if God had gifted me with some beauty and much wealth, I should have made it as hard for you to leave me, as it is now for me to leave you."

**[2]** The wind rumbled through the old trees of Thornfield Hall, but Jane did not look away. She braced herself against the cold evening air, her voice trembling but full of an unyielding determination. "I am not talking to you now through the medium of custom, conventionalities, nor even of mortal flesh;—it is my spirit that addresses your spirit; just as if both had passed through the grave, and we stood at God’s feet, equal,—as we are!"

**[3]** Mr. Rochester stared at her in complete awe, his grim expression softening as he looked into her fierce, flashing eyes. He reached out to grip her arm, but she did not retreat. She stood her ground on the gravel path, refusing to let him treat her like an inferior being. For the first time, the severe barriers of social class and wealth between them seemed to melt away under the relentless power of her words.
