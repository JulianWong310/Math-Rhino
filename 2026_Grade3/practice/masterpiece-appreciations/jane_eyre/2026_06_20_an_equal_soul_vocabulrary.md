* automaton (机器人 / 自动机) A machine that moves and acts by itself without feelings.
  * He worked like an automaton, repeating the same mechanical movements all day.
  * Modern factories use automated systems where every machine operates like a precise automaton.


* morsel (一小块食物) A very small piece of food.
  * The hungry dog swallowed every delicious morsel of meat in seconds.
  * She was so nervous before the exam that she couldn't eat a single morsel of breakfast.


* snatched (猛夺 / 抓走) Taken away quickly and rudely.
  * The thief snatched her purse and vanished into the crowded subway station.
  * He quickly snatched the last slice of pizza before anyone else could reach it.


* dashed (猛掷 / 击碎) Thrown or smashed violently against something.
  * The giant waves dashed the small wooden boat against the sharp rocks.
  * All their hopes of winning the championship were dashed after the final whistle.


* obscure (默默无闻的) Not well-known, humble, or hidden from notice.
  * The famous actor started his career in an obscure little theater group.
  * He was born in an obscure village that was not even marked on the map.

* conventionalities (世俗常规 / 陈规) Traditional social rules that people are expected to follow.
  * She decided to ignore social conventionalities and pursue her own career path.
  * Their wedding was very simple and completely free from traditional conventionalities.


* ensnares (捕捉 / 陷害) Catches or traps someone or something in a net or snare.
  * Spider webs are perfectly designed structures that ensnare flying insects.
  * The clever scam artist tries to ensnare innocent people in his financial traps.


* exert (运用 / 行使) To use power, effort, or influence to make something happen.
  * You need to exert all your strength if you want to push this heavy car out of the mud.
  * Parents often exert a significant influence on their children's reading habits.


* solitary (孤独的 / 孤零零的) Being completely alone; without any companions.
  * The old lighthouse keeper lived a very solitary life on the isolated island.
  * We saw a solitary traveler walking slowly across the empty desert.


* unsustained (孤立无援的) Not supported, comforted, or helped by anyone else.
  * Without any clear evidence, his wild accusations remained completely unsustained.
  * He felt lonely and unsustained when his team members refused to back his proposal.


* sanctioned (经法律或道德认可的) Formally approved or accepted by law or society.
  * The international tournament was officially sanctioned by the world sports federation.
  * This medical procedure is only allowed if it is fully sanctioned by the health committee.


* sane (神志清醒的) Mentally healthy, rational, and clear-headed.
  * No sane person would drive a car at that dangerous speed through heavy rain.
  * Staying calm and sane during a chaotic emergency is a vital skill for firefighters.


* mutiny (叛乱 / 反抗) An open rebellion against authority or strict rules.
  * The cruel treatment by the captain led the sailors to stage a violent mutiny.
  * The strict teacher faced a small mutiny when the entire class refused to do homework.


* rigour (严厉 / 严格) The quality of being extremely severe, strict, or harsh.
  * The scientific experiment was conducted with the highest level of academic rigour.
  * Travelers must prepare carefully to survive the incredible rigour of a Canadian winter.


* preconceived (预想的 / 先入为主的) Formed as an opinion before having real proof or experience.
  * Try to listen to his speech without any preconceived ideas about his background.
  * Her preconceived opinions about the city were completely wrong once she actually visited it.


* foregone (预先注定的 / 过去的) Decided or settled completely beforehand.
  * Since our team was leading by twenty points, the victory became a foregone conclusion.
  * The outcome of the election felt like a foregone decision long before voting day.
