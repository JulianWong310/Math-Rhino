**trailing**（追踪、尾随、拖曳）：Following the scent, track, or path of someone or something.
* The hunting dog kept trailing the scent of the rabbit through the thick forest.
* The boat left a long white wave trailing behind it in the calm water.

**mutilated**（严重残缺的、毁伤的）：Severely damaged, torn, or ruined, especially a body.
* The historic documents were badly mutilated after the library flooded.
* The ancient statue was mutilated over the centuries, losing its nose and arms.

**encounter**（遭遇、意外相见）：A sudden, unexpected, or violent meeting with someone or something.
* The hikers had a scary encounter with a wild bear on the mountain trail.
* This was her first close encounter with a venomous snake in the countryside.

**hesitate**（犹豫、踌躇）：To pause before doing something because you are unsure, worried, or nervous.
* Please do not hesitate to ask if you need any help with your math homework.
* He started to hesitate when the teacher asked him to step onto the stage.

**wallowed**（打滚、在水中翻滚）：Rolled about or lay in water, mud, or waves in a lazy or helpless way.
* The massive hippos wallowed in the muddy river to stay cool under the hot sun.
* The broken ship wallowed helplessly in the giant waves during the storm.

**thrashed**（剧烈扭动、猛烈拍打）：Moved around violently, violently beating the water or ground.
* The big fish thrashed wildly on the deck of the boat trying to get back into the sea.
* The wounded animal thrashed around in the bushes before finally calming down.

**ominous**（不祥的、有威胁的）：Giving a worrying sign that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen.
* Those dark, ominous clouds mean that a massive thunderstorm is on the way.
* A sudden, ominous silence fell over the forest right before the earthquake.

**raw hands**（脱皮流血的手、红肿受损的手）：Hands with skin that is painfully red, rubbed off, or bleeding from hard work.
* After rowing the heavy wooden boat for ten hours, he had raw hands full of blisters.
* The worker washed his raw hands gently after chopping firewood all afternoon.

**assault**（攻击、猛袭）：A violent, sudden, or fierce physical attack on someone or something.
* The soldiers managed to defend the small fort against the enemy's midnight assault.
* The beachfront houses suffered a terrible assault from the hurricane winds.

**scavenger**（食腐动物、捡破烂的人）：An animal or bird that feeds on dead organic matter or scraps left by others.
* The vulture is a well-known scavenger that searches for dead animals from high above.
* Raccoons act like a scavenger in the city, tipping over bins to look for leftover food.

**carcass**（动物尸体）：The dead body of an animal, especially one that has been killed for food or by predators.
* The wolves left the clean bones of the deer carcass scattered across the white snow.
* The fishermen saw a large whale carcass floating in the middle of the open ocean.

**blow**（重击、猛击）：A hard hit with a hand, weapon, or heavy object.
* The champion boxer knocked out his opponent with a single, powerful blow to the jaw.
* The fallen tree branch landed with a heavy blow right on top of the car roof.

**strike**（击打、碰撞）：To hit something or someone forcefully with a weapon, tool, or hand.
* Make sure you do not strike your thumb when using that heavy hammer.
* The captain warned the crew that the ship might strike a hidden rock in the shallow water.

**indifferent**（冷漠的、漠不关心的）：Having no interest, care, or sympathy towards someone's situation or feelings.
* The harsh winter weather seemed completely indifferent to the struggles of the poor birds.
* He knocked on the door for help, but the crowd passing by remained totally indifferent.
