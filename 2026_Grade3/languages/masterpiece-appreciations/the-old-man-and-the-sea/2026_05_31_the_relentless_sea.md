## The Relentless Sea

**[1]** It was an hour before the second shark hit him. This was a wide, shovel-nosed shark, built for eating and trailing the scent of blood in the water. When the old man saw him coming, he knew this was a relentless enemy. He did not look at the fish trailing behind the skiff, already severely mutilated by the first attack. He looked only at the shark. He felt a wave of grim determination settle over him as he prepared for the brutal encounter. 

**[2]** The shark closed in fast, its great jaws opening to reveal rows of sharp, triangular teeth. Santiago did not hesitate. He braced his feet against the floorboards, raised his harpoon with raw hands that throbbed with pain, and struck down with all his remaining strength. He drove the iron point deep into the shark’s head, just between the eyes. The shark wallowed, rolled over, and then thrashed violently on the surface of the water, catching the line in its teeth before it finally turned belly up and sank into the dark ocean.

**[3]** Santiago wiped the salt and sweat from his eyes. His body was aching, and he knew more sharks would come as the scent of blood spread further. He adjusted his grip on the wooden oar, ready to defend what was left of his noble catch. By the time the sun began to dip below the horizon, the water around the skiff had turned a deep, ominous red. The wind started to pick up, carrying with it the cold chill of the approaching night. Santiago could feel the skin on his back tightening from the dried salt, and his throat burned with a fierce thirst. He looked down at his bleeding palms and realized he only had a single oar left to use as a weapon.

**[4]** As the shadows lengthened, the old man heard the terrifying sound of a tail breaking the surface just a few yards away. This time, it was not a single shark, but two more phantoms of the deep moving silently through the dark water. They came from a direction he could not easily defend, their dark fins slicing through the water like knives. His body was nearly broken, his muscles were stiff, and he knew he had no chance of saving the entire fish. Yet, a fierce spark of pride kept him upright, refusing to let him crawl into the bottom of the boat.

**[5]** He braced his body against the wooden stern, gripped his broken oar with his raw hands, and stared directly into the darkness, waiting for the final, relentless assault. When the first scavenger struck the carcass of the prize fish, Santiago swung the oar down with a heavy blow. He felt the wood strike solid bone, and the shock vibrations traveled straight up his arms, splitting his open wounds further. He knew the fight was no longer about saving the meat; it was a pure test of human endurance against the vast, indifferent power of nature.
