* excessively (极度地 / 过分地) To a much greater degree or extent than what is normal or reasonable.
  * The young prince was excessively proud of his luxury sports car collection.
  * She exercises excessively every single day, which makes her feel exhausted.


* accustomed (习惯的 / 适应的) Familiar with something so that it seems normal or usual.
  * After living in Mississauga for five years, she became completely accustomed to the cold winters.
  * He was accustomed to waking up early in the morning to review his vocabulary.


* swindlers (骗子 / 尔虞我诈之人) People who cheat others out of money or property using deception.
  * The clever swindlers set up a fake website to steal innocent people's credit card information.
  * Do not trust those online advertisements, as they might be created by professional swindlers.


* weavers (织布工 / 纺织者) People whose job is to make cloth by crossing threads over and under each other.
  * The traditional weavers spent three months creating this beautiful silk clothing by hand.
  * In the old days, village weavers worked late into the night under small oil lamps.


* looms (织布机) Machines used for weaving thread or yarn into cloth.
  * The factory floor was filled with the loud, rhythmic clicking sounds of modern power looms.
  * They set up two empty looms and pretended to work hard without using any real thread.

* judge (判断 / 评价) To form an opinion or conclusion about something after careful consideration.
  * It is difficult to judge the quality of the material without touching it yourself.
  * You should never judge a person's intelligence based purely on their appearance.


* spectacles (眼镜) A pair of glasses used to correct or assist a person's vision.
  * The old minister adjusted his spectacles so he could read the small print on the paper.
  * He lost his wooden spectacles and spent half an hour searching for them on the desk.


* statesman (政治家 / 国务活动家) An experienced and respected political leader or government official.
  * The retired statesman wrote an insightful book about international relations and trade laws.
  * Every major statesman from the European nations attended the peace conference.


* mantle (斗篷 / 披风) A loose, sleeveless cloak or cape worn over outer garments.
  * The emperor proudly put on his invisible mantle and walked out into the cold street.
  * The royal guard wore a heavy crimson mantle decorated with golden threads.


* canopy (天篷 / 华盖) A decorative cloth covering suspended over a bed, throne, or procession.
  * The servants held a beautiful silk canopy over the king to protect him from the hot sun.
  * The bride walked down the elegant path under a canopy of fresh red roses.


* chamberlains (宫廷侍从 / 内侍) High-ranking officials who manage the living quarters or ceremonies of a king.
  * The royal chamberlains stood in a straight line, waiting to open the grand palace doors.
  * Two noble chamberlains stretched out their hands to carry the long train of the royal robe.
