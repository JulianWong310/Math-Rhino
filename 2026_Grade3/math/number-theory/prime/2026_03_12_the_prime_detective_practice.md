# 🏆 Number Theory Detective

**1. Which of the following numbers is prime?**
* (A) 91
* (B) 117
* (C) 127
* (D) 143
* (E) 201

**2. According to Theorem 1, if we want to determine if the number 397 is prime, what is the largest prime number we MUST test as a divisor?**
* (A) 13
* (B) 17
* (C) 19
* (D) 23
* (E) 31

**3. If $p$ is a prime number and $p$ divides 24, which of the following is NOT a possible value for $p$?**
* (A) 2
* (B) 3
* (C) 4
* (D) All are possible
* (E) None of these

**4. Given that $\gcd(p, 30) = 1$ and $p$ is a prime greater than 5, which of the following must be true?**
* (A) $p$ is a multiple of 7
* (B) $p$ does not divide 30
* (C) $p$ is even
* (D) $p = 31$
* (E) $p$ is composite

**5. Suppose $n$ is a composite number such that $n < 100$. What is the largest possible value for the smallest prime factor of $n$?**
* (A) 2
* (B) 5
* (C) 7
* (D) 11
* (E) 13

**6. If $p$ is a prime and $p | 14ab$, and we know that $\gcd(p, 14) = 1$ and $p \nmid a$, then which must be true?**
* (A) $p = 2$
* (B) $p = 7$
* (C) $p | b$
* (D) $p | 14$
* (E) $b$ is prime

**7. How many prime numbers $p$ satisfy the property that $(p, 15) = p$?**
* (A) 0
* (B) 1
* (C) 2
* (D) 3
* (E) Infinite

**8. If a number $x$ has no prime factors less than or equal to $\sqrt{x}$ (where $x > 1$), then $x$ must be:**
* (A) An even number
* (B) A composite number
* (C) A prime number
* (D) A perfect square
* (E) Zero

**9. Let $p$ be a prime. If $p | x^2$, which of the following statements must be true?**
* (A) $p | x$
* (B) $p^2 | x$
* (C) $x$ must be prime
* (D) $p = 2$
* (E) $x$ is even

**10. A "Detective Number" is defined as a composite number whose smallest prime factor is 7. What is the smallest "Detective Number" possible?**
* (A) 7
* (B) 14
* (C) 35
* (D) 49
* (E) 77

6. **C**
