#### Level 1: The Divisible Code

**The Puzzle:** Find the **smallest** three-digit positive integer with **all different digits** that is divisible by each of its digits.
*(Hint: It can't have a 0 because you can't divide by 0! Try starting with the 100s...)*

---

#### Level 2: The Subset Product Hunt

**The Scenario:** You have a special set of numbers: $\{1, 2, 3, 7, 13\}$. 
**The Question:** How many **different** numbers can you create by multiplying **two or more** distinct members of this set together?
*(Hint: Does multiplying by 1 change the result? Be careful not to double-count!)*

---

#### Level 3: The Apartment Number Supply

**The Scenario:** A hotel needs to label rooms from **100 to 115** on the first floor and **300 to 315** on the second floor. 
**The Rule:** You can only buy digits in "Complete Packs" (one of each digit $0-9$ per pack).
**The Question:** How many packs must you buy to have enough digits for all the room numbers?
*(Hint: Find out which specific digit (like 0 or 1) is used the most. That digit will tell you how many packs you need.)*

---

####  Level 4: The "Digit 8" Search

**The Question:** How many two-digit positive integers have **at least one 8** as a digit?
*(Strategy: You can count the 80s, then count the numbers ending in 8. Don't count 88 twice!)*

---

#### Level 5: The Prime Product Challenge 

**The Question:** How many **factors** of the number $30$ are also members of the set you can create by multiplying **distinct** prime factors of $210$?
*(Hint: First, find the prime factors of 210. Then see which combinations of them result in a factor of 30.)*
